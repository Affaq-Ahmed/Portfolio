After completing my Intermediate from **Fauji Foundation College**, I entered another waiting phase — this time for my **2nd year results**.  
But I didn’t want to sit idle, so I joined **KIPS Academy** to prepare for university entry tests.

I appeared for **NUST, GIKI, UET, ITU**, and **FAST** — all reputed universities in Pakistan.  
I got admission offers from all except NUST (where I was offered other disciplines but not Computer Science).  
The first result that came was from **GIKI**, and I still remember that moment — I was *over the moon*. Then came **ITU**, **FAST**, and **UET**.

The **entry test at FAST was surprisingly easy for me** — I scored **93/100**, and as far as I asked around, those were among the highest marks that year. That confidence boost set the tone for my university journey.

## Choosing My Path

Choosing where to go was tough.  
**GIKI** had its charm, but at the last moment, I made the call to join **FAST-NUCES, Lahore** — a decision that, looking back, shaped my life in more ways than I could have imagined.

FAST is known for its “**Ragra**” — the intense load of quizzes, assignments, projects, and exams that define its academic culture.  
It’s not just about intelligence there; it’s about **consistency, pressure-handling, and time management**.  
Those four years tested me in every way, and I’m grateful they did.

## Early Setback — The Wake-Up Call

In my **second semester**, I faced my first real setback.  
An **OOP assignment** I submitted was marked plagiarized because my friends and I had collaborated too closely — even though it was supposed to be a solo task.  
I had to **redo the entire OOP course during summer**.

At that time, it felt harsh. But in hindsight, that moment changed me.  
It made me take university seriously — and taught me ownership, integrity, and accountability.

## The Courses That Shaped Me

From that point forward, I gave my best to every course.  
Some of the subjects where I truly excelled were:

- **Programming Fundamentals (PF)**  
- **Object-Oriented Programming (OOP)**  
- **Databases (DB)**  
- **Computer Networks**  
- **Principles of Leadership**  
- **Creative Writing**  
- **Entrepreneurship**  
- **Software Design and Analysis**  
- **Theory of Automata**  
- **Artificial Intelligence (AI)**  
- **Cloud Computing**  
- **Web Programming**

These weren’t just subjects — they were puzzle pieces of what I’d eventually become.  
I especially loved courses that blended logic with creativity — *OOP, Web Programming,* and *AI* stood out the most.

## Falling in Love with Code

University was where I truly **fell in love with programming**.  
I learned **C++**, **SQL**, a bit of **Python**, and yes — **Java**, which I *hated with all my guts* 😅.  
But when I discovered **JavaScript** in our Web Programming course, it clicked instantly.  

It felt fast, dynamic, and alive — just like me.  
That’s when I decided: *this is what I want to master*.  
It became the core stack of my **Final Year Project**, which I built using **Node.js**, **React.js**, and **React Native** — something I’ll share in detail in another post.

## The FAST Experience

Life at FAST wasn’t easy.  
There were nights before finals where I went completely blank, and others where I felt fully prepared — but somehow, I always managed to push through.  
FAST taught me how to **sustain pressure**, stay **disciplined**, and remain **resilient**.

From long project submissions to 2 midterms, weekly quizzes, and labs — every bit of that *“Ragra”* built me into a stronger, more capable version of myself.  
It taught me that excellence isn’t a one-time effort — it’s a habit.

## A Reputation That Follows

Whenever I mention **FAST-NUCES Lahore**, people nod with respect — because they know what it stands for.  
That’s what the university gave me — **reputation, resilience, and respect**.

## Looking Back

From choosing Computer Science to spending sleepless nights debugging, every experience shaped me.  
FAST gave me **direction**, **confidence**, and **endurance** — and most importantly, it helped me realize that I’m not just a student of technology…  
I’m a **student for life**.
